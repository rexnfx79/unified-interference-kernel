# Manuscript — Honest Limitations Paragraph (draft for paste)

**Suggested placement:** end of Introduction or start of Discussion/Limitations.

---

This work treats the interference kernel as a **phenomenological parameterization** of Standard Model Yukawa textures, not as a derived QFT mechanism. Pre-registered diagnostics in the companion repository refute an operational quantum-information-theoretic explanation of sector-specific parameter splits (Fisher alignment 0.46, frozen quark-to-lepton transfer loss \(\gtrsim 800\), pooled open-system correlations \(|r| \lesssim 0.01\)). **Universal kernel parameters across fermion sectors are ruled out**; only the functional form is reused.

For the **quark sector**, optimization on training targets (\(m_c\), \(|V_{us}|\), \(|V_{cb}|\)) does not generalize to holdout observables (\(m_u\), \(m_d\), \(m_s\), \(|V_{ub}|\)): the structural CKM–\(m_c\) Pareto trade-off persists across Gaussian, clockwork, and generalized envelopes, and **no geometry achieves simultaneous agreement with all quoted PDG targets** at pre-registered tolerances (0% strict survivor rate). Clockwork envelopes can improve training loss relative to Gaussian on some geometries but do not resolve the sector failure.

Charged-lepton and neutrino results are **sector-specific fits** with archived survivor rates; they must not be read as validation of a three-regime sampling hypothesis while the quark sector fails. Arithmetic links (Riemann zeros, primes) are explicitly **out of scope** for flavor predictions in this paper.
