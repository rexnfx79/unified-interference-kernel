# Split-Fermion Localization

## Established (EFT / extra dimensions)

In split-fermion models (Arkani-Hamed–Schmaltz and descendants), SM fermions are localized at separated points (or narrow wavepackets) along a compact or semi-infinite extra dimension. Yukawa couplings arise from **overlap integrals** of left- and right-handed zero-mode wavefunctions — a standard Hilbert-space construction on \(L^2\) of the internal space.

## Proven in physics literature (not in this repo's code)

- Overlap suppression with separation explains **hierarchical** Yukawa eigenvalues.
- Geometric distances map to exponentiated suppression (often Gaussian or exponential in distance).
- Does **not** automatically produce full CKM/PMNS structure without additional phases or multiple fields.
