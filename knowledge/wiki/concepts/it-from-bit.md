# It from Bit

## Summary

John Wheeler's **"It from Bit"** (1990): physical quantities derive meaning from yes/no answers — information is fundamental in the **participatory** sense.

## Status in This Wiki

**philosophical** — not a theorem, not a Lagrangian. North star for [[information-creates-reality]], but **cannot** bridge to flavor or primes alone.

## What It Provides

- Motivation for [[quantum-information]] and measurement-as-information
- Conceptual alignment with holographic bounds (Bekenstein, 't Hooft, Susskind)

## What It Does **Not** Provide

- Self-adjoint operator for zeta zeros
- Yukawa matrices
- Falsifiable mass predictions

## Constructive Path (Required)

Wheeler → **operationalize** via [[qed-qm-information]]:

1. Define measurable information quantities in QED
2. Derive constraints on couplings
3. Only then ask if arithmetic appears ([[primes-via-quantum-effects]])
