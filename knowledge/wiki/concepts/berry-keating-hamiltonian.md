# Berry–Keating Hamiltonian

## Proposal

Berry and Keating (1999) suggest the classical Hamiltonian

\[
H = xp
\]

(particle on the half-line with \(p = -i\hbar \partial/\partial x\)) has semiclassical quantization whose energy levels relate to Riemann zeros:

\[
E_n \sim \frac{2\pi n}{\log n} \quad \text{(heuristic)}
\]

## Significance

- Concrete **candidate** linking quantum mechanics to [[riemann-zeta-function]] zeros
- Motivates [[hilbert-polya-conjecture]] from physics side
- Connects to quantum chaos: closed orbits, trace formulas

## Problems

- \(H = xp\) is not self-adjoint on standard \(L^2\) domains without careful extension
- Full correspondence with *all* zeros not established
- Not yet a proof path to RH

## Bridge to This Project

If a renormalized \(xp\)-type operator underlies arithmetic spectra, **effective** flavor operators ([[interference-kernel]]) might be dimensionally reduced versions — highly speculative; see [[spectral-interpretation-of-flavor]].
