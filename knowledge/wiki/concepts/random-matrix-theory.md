# Random Matrix Theory

## Proven

- GUE spacing statistics for chaotic quantum systems and for zeta zeros — **pair correlation proven (conditional on RH)**; full GUE not proven ([[montgomery-pair-correlation]])

## Conjecture

- Yukawa eigenvalue spacing might follow a RMT class — **unknown**; not validated in repo.

## Refuted

- 3×3 GUE test proposed for flavor matrices — **dead** (only two spacings) per [[plausibility-register]].

## Summary

Random Matrix Theory (RMT) describes eigenvalue statistics of large random matrices. The **Gaussian Unitary Ensemble (GUE)** gives spacing distributions for chaotic quantum systems and — remarkably — for [[riemann-zeta-function]] zero spacings.

## Key Results

- **Montgomery (1973)**: pair correlation of zeta zeros matches GUE **under RH** — [[montgomery-pair-correlation]] (not full n-point GUE)
- **Odlyzko**: massive numerical confirmation of GUE statistics for zeros
- **Bohigas–Giannoni–Schmit**: chaotic spectra follow RMT

RMT is the **strongest established bridge** between arithmetic (zeta) and spectral (quantum) worlds. Any claim that [[interference-kernel]] connects to zeta must respect GUE universality — predictions should match spacing statistics, not just individual eigenvalues.
